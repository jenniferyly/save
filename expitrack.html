About

Expitrack is a mobile application concept to help people remember to use their food through the use of a pet like in-app companion. By tapping into the potential of humans' nuturing nature towards cute things, this app is made to motivate the user to actively track their food purchases to help their companion stay healthy and grow. Originally made by team members Shuhei Tsuriya, Alexandr Iakovlev, Jennifer Ly, and Tianyi Liu for UC Davis 2020 Winter Quarter ECS 164: Human Computer Interaction course project.

The Problem

Food waste is a serious issue in the United States. People waste billions of pounds of food due to many reasons including but not limited to refusal to eat leftovers, not knowing what to cook with their produce, or not knowing how to store their food properly. From our research, we found that one of the biggest reasons for food waste is forgetting expiration dates.

The Solution

Our team decided to design an app that users can track expiration dates in while adding a fun element to give further incentive for users to more actively use it. By having a digital companion users can play with and care for, users will be more likely to check in on the app and be more aware of their food and expiration dates.

User Research

Our team surveyed 32 UC Davis students to find the cause of people wasting food and found the biggest reason is that people tend to forget the expiration dates and they do nothing to prevent it. From the survey, we found that 50% of people waste food because they forget the expiration dates and even 25% of people do nothing to avoid forgetting them. From these results, it can be said that the problem is that some people do not care about the expiration dates and they waste the expired food.

User Testing

Note: while the full original high fidelity prototype has been overwritten and is not displayed, the user testing was conducted on said prototype

Our team conducted five user tests by giving the prototype to the users to navigate without help or instructions from the designers. This helped us pinpoint areas that needed improvement.

The majority of the users stated they did not understand how to use the app or the menu was unclear. Our team decided to add a tutorial for when the user first launches the app that can be brought back up again at anytime by clicking a question mark button that was also added to the home screen. We also added labels to the navigation menu items. Many users also reported that the graph on the statistics screen was hard to understand due to the lack of units. Lastly, users said the app was not fun to play. Part of the team decided to remedy this by adding a mini-game portion. I personally thought that there was miscommunication in the "marketing" of the app as it's not meant to be a gaming app or an app you actively play on, but rather an app meant to have a passive fun component.

Features

Virtual Companion

A character that lives on the home screen of the app. They are there the show the status of your food wasting habits. The more food you throw away, the weaker and sickly it will look.

Virtual Currency

You can gain coins from logging food into the app. These coins can be used to customize your character. This is system is meant to encourage users to remember to log the food they buy.

Barcode Scanner

To make logging your food easier, the app contains a barcode scanner that uses the phone camera to detect the barcode and pull the necessary data associated with said barcode.

Food List

The app stores all the food you've inputted in a list organized by expiration dates. There's also a visual cue for the expiration date, the sooner the expiration date, the more red the item will appear on the list.

Statistics

The app will keep track of your food usage habits based on whether food is thrown out or not. If you also inputted the price of your food item, it will also calculate your spending habits in regard to food.

Additional/Future Concepts and Features

Some features I'd like to incorporate if I decide to fully develop this app.

Food Database

Ideally, the app will be connected to a database of various food products so it can auto fill the expiration date of items that don't have a set expiration date (ie. fresh produce) by searching for the average shelf life. Additionally, the database could contain suggested storage methods for different types of foods and the app can suggest such methods to increase shelf life.

Recipe Suggestions

If the user can't figure out what to do with their food item, the app can suggest recipes containing the main selected item. If possible, I'd like it to take into account other items on the list whenever possible and have the recipes with the most items on the list show up first.

Food Safety Information

A disclaimer should be included whenever the app suggests an average shelf life expiration date and link to a section in the app with information about when food should be thrown out even if it's not past its "expiration" date yet along with other important food safety information, such as which produce it is safe or not to just cut mold off of and be safe to still use the non-moldy part.
